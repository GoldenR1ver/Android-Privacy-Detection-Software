Data protection
Data protection is a high priority at SwitzerlandMobility. Users of the SwitzerlandMobility websites or apps decide what personal information they wish to disclose. By uploading location-related information and photos, in particular, personal content is uploaded to our servers; it is neither evaluated by us in terms of content nor shared with third parties. However, we wish to point out that through the visit of our websites and apps or reading the newsletters, we automatically obtain information that enables us to determine how visitors use our websites, apps and newsletters and what information and offers are of particular interest. If visitors make reservations through our websites, register for a SwitzerlandMobility Plus subscription, request information material or other services, we need personal information such as the name and address. Insofar as such services are provided by third parties, the details will be forwarded to the respective service providers, an action to which we are automatically authorised by the provision of the details. In all other respects, personal information is handled confidentially and processed by us or passed to third parties in anonymous form only. Such third parties may also be outside Switzerland. Data processing performed by us or by third parties is carried out, with the exception of providing the requested services, solely for the purpose of individual information of our users, in line with their interests and requirements as well as for our statistical and marketing purposes and for tourism service providers in Switzerland. Data processing is intended to enable us to align our websites and our service offers with the requirements of the users. Data processing is also carried out in particular for marketing analysis and to determine the interest in the multifaceted tourism services offered in Switzerland so as to conclude how to shape these tourism services to meet demands and to incorporate them into the Swiss tourism operations. Any other use of personal information, in particular the sale to third parties, is explicitly excluded. Registered users can request information on the content of their customer data. They may request deletion of all of their data. In this case, please contact: info@switzerlandmobility.ch (opens in a new tab)
Technical tracking and analysis data
For site optimisation and statistics purposes, we collect tracking data, which gives information about the usage behaviour of users. The tracking data we collect for Google Analytics does not allow conclusions to be drawn of individual users. No one can be identified through the use of such data. This website uses Google Analytics, a web analytics service provided by Google Inc. ("Google"). The apps use Google Analytics, Google Analytics for Firebase and Crashlytics for Firebase. Google Analytics uses cookies, i.e. text files which are stored on the computer and enable the analysis of the user's use of the website. The information generated by the cookies about the use of the website (including the IP address) is transmitted to and stored in a Google server in the USA. Google uses this information to analyse the use of the website, to compile reports on website activity for the website operators and to provide other services related to the use of website and internet. Google may also transfer this information to third parties if required to do so by law or where third parties process these data on behalf of Google. Under no circumstances will Google associate the personal IP address with other data held by Google. The installation of cookies can be prevented by selecting the appropriate settings in the browser software; however, we wish to point out that in this case it may not be possible to use all the features of this website. By using the digital services of SwitzerlandMobility, users agree to the processing of their data collected by Google in the manner described above and for the aforementioned purpose. 
Cookies
Cookies are small data elements that a website can send to the browser in order to provide the user with better support for database-supported systems. It is possible to configure the browser such that it informs the user when a cookie is received or to automatically block it. Therefore, the users can decide if they want to accept the configuration or not. However, the result of failing to accept cookies is the inability to establish a personal account.
